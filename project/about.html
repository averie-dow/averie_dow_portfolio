CU Boulder Safe

My most recently completed project was for CU Boulder Safe, which is a CU community safety application for students, faculty, and staff. We worked closely with the CU Boulder Police Department to conduct initial interviews and further user research on the usability of the application as it initially came from its parent company, AppArmor. While I was involved in the User Research, I was primarily focused on the design. I used data collected from usability tests to find the most easily recognized iconography for each of the available safety functions on CU Boulder Safe.


Life!

World of Vikings

I am fresh off of my 2025 Summer Abroad in Denmark, Iceland, and Sweden with DIS Abroad! While I was abroad I took three courses to fulfill some elective credits, including Norse Mythology, World of Vikings, and Women in the Viking Age. No, I was not REQUIRED to take only viking-related courses. It was my pleasure. One of the most interesting parts I learned about Scandinavian culture in the Viking Age was that women actually had a fairly autonomous role in society and were able to divorce, own property, and, in some cases (and with much debate in the community) go on viking. Additionally, the Scandinavian raiders were not "Vikings" they were farmers, traders, etc. who went "on viking." "To go on viking" actually refers to anyone who travels a distance to retrieve goods or learn something. So technically while I was abroad, I was on viking.
